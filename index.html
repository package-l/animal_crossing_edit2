Animal Crossing: New Horizons

2.0 Update

Get Started

What are we looking at?

Per its Wikipedia page, Animal Crossing: New Horizons grossed $654 million in global digital sales by the end of 2020, making it the year's seventh highest-grossing digital premium game worldwide. As of March 31, 2021, the game has sold 32.63 million units. Because of the timing of the release around the start of the pandemic, the game not only was popular in terms of sales, but it allowed for a very interactive community of people stuck in their homes to come together from all over the world and help each other out in building great islands.

With the highly anticipated 2.0 game update set to premiere November 5th, we wanted to take a look to see if the already highly active community was reinvigorated and also explore what they were talking most about!

Let's take a deeper look into the Animal Crossing community! When examining the community, it is important to not only understand when things are being said or how popular any one statement may be. It is important to look at the community as a whole!
Blathers

To do this, we tapped into the emoji use of the community to get a sense of what else is being communicated around the time of the big update. Emojis can reveal a lot about the users, from getting an overall sentiment/mood gauge regarding the game to what things people are adding to their island. In particular, the Animal Crossing community seems to like to use emojis to reflect current events in the game as well as other available game features such as flowers, plants, crop growing, and food.

Our team observed that the jack-o-lantern emoji was used most frequently. This aligns with our expectations as the tweets encompass the day of Halloween! When examining the relationships between different emojis, we also noticed that for some reason, out of the two available red heart emojis, one heart emoji was strongly favored in terms of overall usage, yet the other was used much more frequently in the same tweet as other emojis. We were also happy to see that the coffee cup was a commonly used emoji. It seems like the community is very excited about Brewster's Cafe being added to the game through the November 4th update! Click the NEXT button to look through the emoji usage during the week and make your own observations! Hover and click the buttons to explore more on your own!

So what does the Twitter community for Animal Crossing look like since the update?

As you can see, the Animal Crossing community is still very active on social media platforms. As you may have gleaned from some of the visualizations above, there seemed to be a lot of activity starting on November 3rd and November 4th, prior to the stated release date. Well, that is because it dropped early! According to Polygon and many of the Twitter users as well, the release surprisingly came out about a day earlier than expected. Did you notice it in your explorations?

By tracing the activity of the Animal Crossing community on Twitter, it seems apparent to our team that fans of the game are both excited and very happy about the update! The update seems to have only increased excitement in the game and made the already active community more active on Twitter. The engagement fans have with each other about the game is extremely lively and overwhelmingly positive. We challenge you to find a single tweet with negative sentiment. Our team thinks it's a great time to pick up the game, start playing, and join the community!
